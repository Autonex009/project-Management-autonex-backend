# Autonex Slack Etiquette & Guidelines

## 1. Core Principles
- **Asynchronous Communication**: Respect that colleagues may be in deep work. Don't expect immediate replies unless it's a critical emergency.
- **Public by Default**: Keep project discussions in public channels rather than Direct Messages (DMs) so knowledge is searchable and accessible to the team.

## 2. Do's
- **Use Threads**: Always reply in threads to keep channels organized.
- **Status Updates**: Keep your Slack status updated (e.g., In a meeting, OOO, Focusing).
- **Search First**: Before asking a question, use Slack search to see if it's already been answered.
- **Provide Context**: When asking for help, provide all necessary links, screenshots, and context upfront. "Hello" without context is discouraged.

## 3. Don'ts
- **No @channel or @here unless critical**: Use these tags sparingly, as they notify everyone across all timezones.
- **Don't DM for general questions**: Post in the relevant project or `#help` channel instead.
- **Avoid "Ping Pong"**: If a discussion goes back and forth more than 5 times, hop on a quick huddle or call.
